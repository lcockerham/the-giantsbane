I've been running a high level 5e game in Planescape, and converting a number of the old adventures. Many of the adventures are great, but they tend towards being very hard to run for a number of reasons:

-   The adventures are old, and adventure design has improved a lot in the intervening years.
-   Planescape is a complicated setting. It mashed together pieces from multiple other settings, plus it has all of its own complicated lore.
-   The narrative design of Planescape adventures is usually rooted in the social interaction pillar of Dungeons & Dragons. I like an adventure to explore multiple pillars of gameplay, so I tend to try to find ways to shoehorn in combat encounters.
-   On top of all that, I am running at tier 3 and 4, and these adventures really seem to have tier 2, maybe 3, in mind. That's a good thing for making the adventure accessible to more players, but it makes it even harder to convert them.

As I have been doing my conversions, Sean McGovern's [Guide to Planescape Factions](http://thecampaign20xx.blogspot.com/2016/10/dungeons-dragons-guide-to-factions-of.html) has been an invaluable tool. So I have taken his work and condensed it down to the table below that DMs can use for a quick reference. You can also get a printable [PDF](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/e/2PACX-1vR1qfJeFhZSYd4HWkAXt5Mrrd5kdfDuSp6Gw_rIQNHeWUHuz2xwXThRVH3kFq7DMrrnGLoVJzo7851f/pub?output=pdf) of the table.
