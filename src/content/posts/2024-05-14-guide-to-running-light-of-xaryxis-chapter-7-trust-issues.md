## Chapter 7: Trust Issues

I used this chapter for character development. I let the characters know up front all of the NPCs they could talk to after an initial conversation with the Princess. I then let each character choose an NPC they want to speak with one on one. Once everyone had spoken with one person, I ran an event with Topolah and a Deck of Many Things, then I let social encounters continue until we neared the end of the session. Once we reached the end, I made the scene with Warwyck escaping from the aartuks the cliffhanger.

-   It will take 21 days for the Second Wind to reach its destination in Doomspace.

-   Princess Xedalli
    -   Her twin, Prince Xeleth, ordered the attack on the character's home world under orders from her dying father, Emperor Xavan.
    -   When the timing is appropriate, read her box text.
    -   Xedalli is purely motivated by vengeance.
    -   Xedalli pretends to be rational and compromising but would sooner die than surrender her claim to the throne.
    -   Xedalli vows to save Faerun if the characters impress her, but she doesn't take the vow very seriously.
    -   Xedalli wears a **_ring of shooting stars._**
    -   Xedalli never reveals her ability to summon a Solar Dragon.
    -   Things she will share with the characters:
        -   Xedalli and Xeleth were meant to share the throne. (true)
        -   Emperor Xavan wants to become one with Light of Xaryxis when it is at its brightest, thus the attack on Faerun to siphon energy and brighten the star. (true)
        -   The only way to save Faerun is to destroy the star Xaryxis. (true)
        -   A member of the royal family can destroy Xaryxis by performing a ritual at the astral front in the Temple of Light in the Imperial Fortress. (false — anyone with a **_ring of shooting stars_** can perform the ritual)
        -   The Imperial fortress will orbit the star until Xavan ascends, then the fortress will return to the Astral Sea. (true)

-   NPC Interactions during travel
    -   Commodore Krux
        -   Krux is sulking because he tried to destroy the Imperial Fortress some time ago, but his fleet was routed.
        -   Xeleth's solar dragon did massive damage to his ship. He turned tail and ran, and it embarrasses him.
        -   DC 13 Charisma (Persuasion) check to get him to buck up.
        -   Krux will tell characters they need to find old mate Warwyck Blastimoff, who left for Doomspace to join the anti-Xaryxian coalition.
        -   Krux and Warwyck were communicating regularly before Krux lost his sending stone on Bral.
        -   When they last spoke, Warwyck was in Doomspace, on the moon of Aruun, looking for allies.
        -   Once he is out of his melancholy, things to do with Krux:
            -   Shoot guns (opposed ranged attack rolls)
            -   Tell war stories (Charisma (Performance) checks)
    -   Gambling with Grimzod Gargenhale
        -   Use the rules from [Dead Hand's Dice](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/tbomt/jester#FortuneTelling) in Turn of Fortune's Wheel
        -   Each participant's player chooses any number of d6s (known as "bones") and shakes them in a cup.
        -   You choose any number of d6s as well, and roll for the dealer and participating NPCs.
        -   Everyone rolls and reveals their dice at once.
        -   The participant with the highest total wins, but anyone who rolls a 1 automatically loses.
    -   Deck of Many Things with Topolah
        -   Have Topolah perform a fortune telling using the cards from the Deck of Many Things. You can find details on how to do this in the [Fortune Telling](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/tbomt/jester#FortuneTelling) section of Chapter 14 in the Book of Many Things.
        -   Topolah will also allow characters to draw cards from the deck if they so choose. This can be game breaking, though the Book of Many Things does a good job of giving DMs the tools to deal with negative outcomes.
    -   Sails and Styles with Mister Flinch
        -   Characters can help Mister Flinch mend sails.
        -   Alternatively, Mister Flinch will pointedly critique any character's style as long as they are willing to hear his candid critiques.
    -   Very little with Starbough
        -   Starbough will mostly ignore the characters.
        -   Repeated attempts, along with signaling interest in Wildspace and Exploration, might eventually get Starbough to warm up to a character.
        -   If he does, Starbough can impart all of the information about Doomspace from later in the chapter.

-   Random encounter ideas
    -   Mage named Ralph Strange traveling via astral projection.
    -   Sleeping cosmic horror.
    -   Monodrone lost from the Great Modron March.
    -   Unicorns merrily dancing across the Astral Sea.

-   Doomspace
    -   Show characters maps of Doomspace.
    -   General backstory: clash with gods led to creation of Eye of Doom. All planets rotate closer and closer to the Eye of Doom; when a planet reaches the Eye it is destroyed.
    -   Fyreen is closest to the eye and has already lost one moon to it. It will lose a second in a month's time.
    -   Fyreen's residents have already been evacuated to the nine moons of En.

-   The Aartuks of Aruun
    -   Read the box text but add a pair of battling Tarrasques in the wasteland. It's the coolest part of Doomspace! A moon overrun with Tarrasques!
    -   Read the box text of Warwyck escaping the Aartuks. I used this as the cliffhanger and started the next session with the combat. I also left out the bulette combat. That worked better for timing for me since we had a full session of roleplaying during travel. Then the next session took us through chapter 8, and featured a lot of combat.
